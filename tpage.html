Sylvia Plath

Poet - Novelist - Muse

"Apparently, the most difficult feat for a Cambridge male is to accept a woman not merely as feeling, not merely as thinking, but as managing a complex, vital interweaving of both." - Sylvia Plath

A timeline of her life

  • 1932 - Born October 27, in Boston, Massachusetts
  • 1953 - Awarded the position as guest editor at Mademoiselle magazine in New York. Attempted suicide after returning from New York City.
  • 1955 - Received Fulbright scholarship to study in Cambridge.
  • 1956 - Married fellow poet Ted Hughes.
  • 1960 - Plath's collection of poems The Colossus was published. Her daughter Frieda was born in the same year.
  • 1961 - Completed her semi-autobiographical novel The Bell Jar
  • 1962 - Son Nicholas was born. Plath separates from Hughes after discovering that he had an affair.
  • 1963 - The Bell Jar was published under the pen name Victoria Lucas, the same year that Plath took her life.
  • 1982 - Posthumously received the Pulitzer Prize for The Collected Poems
"I can never read all the books I want; I can never be all the people I want and live all the lives I want. I can never train myself in all the skills I want. And why do I want? I want to live and feel all the shades, tones and variations of mental and physical experience possible in my life. And I am horribly limited." - Sylvia Plath
